Dyrroth: The Wrath Unleashed - MLBB Guide

Alright, here's a comprehensive MLBB guide for Dyrroth, focusing on his primary and secondary lanes, builds, emblems, talents, and counter items.

Hero Role: Fighter

Primary Lane: EXP Lane

Secondary Lane: Jungle (Less Common, but viable)

Hero Strengths:

  • High Burst Damage: Dyrroth can quickly shred enemy health, especially with his passive and enhanced skills.
  • Good Sustain: He has built-in HP regeneration through his passive and skill effects, making him durable in lane.
  • Strong Early to Mid Game: Dyrroth is a lane bully who excels at applying pressure early on.
  • Anti-Heal: Dyrroth's ultimate reduces healing effects, making him a threat to sustain-heavy teams.

Hero Weaknesses:

  • Mana Hungry Early Game: Dyrroth can quickly run out of mana if you spam skills.
  • Vulnerable to CC: Crowd control can easily shut down Dyrroth's aggression.
  • Falls Off Late Game: While still impactful, Dyrroth's effectiveness diminishes as enemies acquire more armor and HP.
  • Lack of mobility: Dyrroth isn't very agile or mobile.

EXP Lane Dyrroth

Playstyle: Dominate the lane early, harass the enemy laner, and look for opportunities to rotate and help your team. Prioritize building damage and sustain. Remember to manage your mana effectively.

Build 1: Aggressive Bruiser (High Damage)

  • Emblem: Fighter Emblem
    • Talent 1: Bravery (Increased Physical Attack)
    • Talent 2: Swift (Attack Speed)
    • Talent 3: Festival of Blood (Spell Vamp with each kill/assist) - Best for sustain in team fights
  • Items:
    1. / Warrior Boots/Tough Boots: (Choose based on enemy team comp. Warrior for Physical Damage, Tough for Magic Damage/CC)
    2. Bloodlust Axe: (Spell Vamp and CD reduction. Core item for sustain.)
    3. Hunter Strike: (Physical Attack, CD Reduction, and Movement Speed boost after dealing damage. Excellent for chasing and rotating.)
    4. Malefic Roar: (High Physical Penetration. Needed for dealing damage to tanks.)
    5. Brute Force Breastplate: (HP, Physical Defense, and Movement Speed when using skills. Helps with mobility and tankiness.)
    6. Immortality: (Provides a second life. Crucial for late-game survival.)

Explanation: This build focuses on dealing significant damage while also providing sustain and some defense. The spell vamp from Bloodlust Axe and the HP from Brute Force Breastplate allows you to stay in the fight longer. Hunter Strike helps chase enemies or retreat, and Malefic Roar helps kill tanks.

Build 2: Sustain Focused (Durable and Long Fight Potential)

  • Emblem: Fighter Emblem
    • Talent 1: Bravery (Increased Physical Attack)
    • Talent 2: Swift (Attack Speed)
    • Talent 3: Killing Spree (Regain HP and Movement Speed after a kill) - Best for snowballing and escaping
  • Items:
    1. / Warrior Boots/Tough Boots: (Choose based on enemy team comp. Warrior for Physical Damage, Tough for Magic Damage/CC)
    2. Bloodlust Axe: (Spell Vamp and CD reduction. Core item for sustain.)
    3. War Axe: (Physical Attack, HP, CD Reduction. Dealing continuous damage increases physical attack and movement speed.)
    4. Oracle: (Magic Defense, HP, CD Reduction, increases shield absorption and HP regen effect.)
    5. Athena's Shield: (Magic Defense, HP, Reduces magic damage taken. More magic defense and hp.)
    6. Immortality: (Provides a second life. Crucial for late-game survival.)

Explanation: This build sacrifices a bit of damage for increased durability and sustain. War Axe and Oracle provides constant HP regeneration. Athena's Shield help you survive magic burst damage.

EXP Lane Counter Items (Situational):

  • Against High Physical Damage (e.g., Zilong, Martis):
    • Replace Brute Force Breastplate/Athena's Shield with Blade Armor: Reflects a portion of the damage received back to the attacker.
  • Against High Magic Damage (e.g., Yve, Pharsa):
    • Replace Hunter Strike with Athena's Shield: Provides a significant Magic Damage reduction shield.
  • Against High Healing (e.g., Estes, Rafaela, Uranus):
    • Dyrroth's ultimate has anti-heal effect. If the opponent is still healing alot:
    • Sea Halberd: Use it as an additional item if you need more antiheal. Replace Brute Force/Athena.
  • Against CC Heavy Compositions (e.g., Eudora, Aurora):
    • Tough Boots: This can be used if the opposing team consist of multiple CC heroes.
    • Rose Gold Meteor/Great Dragon Spear: Rose Gold Meteor will give you a shield and movement speed when your HP is below 30%. Great Dragon Spear gives increase to CC reduction. Switch the item with Brute Force/Athena.

Jungle Dyrroth

Playstyle: Focus on farming efficiently early, secure objectives (Turtle, Lord), and gank lanes to help your teammates. Build primarily damage to quickly eliminate targets. Prioritize retri use on objectives.

Build 1: Burst Damage Jungler

  • Emblem: Assassin Emblem
    • Talent 1: Agility (Increased Movement Speed)
    • Talent 2: Invasion (Increased Physical Penetration)
    • Talent 3: High and Dry (Increased damage when isolated with an enemy) - Best for 1v1 potential
  • Items:
    1. Hunter's Knife (Jungle Item): Prioritize the Encourage version to help your team with damage or the Flame Retribution to slow enemies.
    2. / Warrior Boots/Tough Boots: (Choose based on enemy team comp. Warrior for Physical Damage, Tough for Magic Damage/CC)
    3. Bloodlust Axe: (Spell Vamp and CD reduction.)
    4. Hunter Strike: (Physical Attack, CD Reduction, and Movement Speed boost after dealing damage.)
    5. Malefic Roar: (High Physical Penetration.)
    6. Blade of Despair: (Massive Physical Attack when hitting enemies with low HP.)
    7. Immortality: (Provides a second life.)

Explanation: This build is all about maximizing damage output for quick kills. The Assassin emblem and Blade of Despair synergize well to burst down enemies. Remember to upgrade the Jungle item to its final form as early as possible.

Build 2: Balanced Jungle Build

  • Emblem: Assassin Emblem
    • Talent 1: Agility (Increased Movement Speed)
    • Tier 2: Invasion (Increased Physical Penetration)
    • Tier 3: Killing Spree (Regain HP and Movement Speed after a kill) - Best for snowballing
  • Items:
    1. Hunter's Knife (Jungle Item): Prioritize the Encourage version to help your team with damage or the Flame Retribution to slow enemies.
    2. / Warrior Boots/Tough Boots: (Choose based on enemy team comp. Warrior for Physical Damage, Tough for Magic Damage/CC)
    3. Bloodlust Axe: (Spell Vamp and CD reduction.)
    4. Hunter Strike: (Physical Attack, CD Reduction, and Movement Speed boost after dealing damage.)
    5. Malefic Roar: (High Physical Penetration.)
    6. Brute Force Breastplate: (HP, Physical Defense, and Movement Speed when using skills. Helps with mobility and tankiness.)
    7. Immortality: (Provides a second life.)

Explanation: This provides sustain while jungling and tankiness to survive when diving the backline.

Jungle Counter Items (Situational):

  • Against High Magic Damage Teams:
    • Replace Blade of Despair/Brute Force Breastplate with Athena's Shield.
  • Against High Armor/Tanks:
    • Consider a second Malefic Roar instead of Immortality (if you are confident in your positioning).
  • Against Crowd Control Heavy Teams:
    • Consider Great Dragon Spear instead of Blade of Despair/Brute Force Breastplate

General Dyrroth Tips & Tricks:

  • Maximize Passive: Dyrroth's passive (Wrath of the Abyss) enhances his skills. Use your skills strategically to trigger the enhanced versions.
  • Skill Combos: A basic combo is Skill 1 -> Skill 2 -> Enhanced Skill 1 -> Enhance Skill 2 -> Ultimate.
  • Mana Management: Be mindful of your mana, especially early game. Don't spam skills unnecessarily.
  • Early Game Bully: Dyrroth is strong early. Harass the enemy laner and look for opportunities to get an advantage.
  • Team Fighting: Focus on targeting squishy backline heroes. Use your ultimate to reduce healing on key targets.
  • Map Awareness: Rotate to help your team when needed. Keep an eye on the map for opportunities to gank.
  • Practice in Classic: Before playing Dyrroth in ranked, practice in classic mode to get comfortable with his skills and playstyle.

Gameplay:

  • Early game: secure your lane by farming and harassing the opponent.
  • Mid game: secure any objective, gank a lane where it's needed.
  • Late game: always be with your team and wait for an opportunity to dive on the backline.
